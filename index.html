A Comprehensive Survey of Medical Imaging Datasets by GMAI

This work constitutes the first large-scale survey of more than 1,000 publicly available medical imaging datasets, with the objective of facilitating the development of robust and generalizable medical foundation models.

Medical imaging datasets represent the cornerstone of AI-driven healthcare; however, they remain fragmented, small in scale, and narrowly task-specific. This survey characterizes modalities, clinical tasks, anatomical coverage, and annotation types, and outlines integrative strategies to maximize their collective utility.

Field guide & examples
  • dimension: one of "2D", "3D", "video". Tip: set allow_3d_as_2d_sources to true if you accept 3D as 2D sources.
  • modalities: array of primary modalities (e.g., ["CT","MR","Fundus"]).
  • task_types: array of clinical tasks (e.g., classification / segmentation / detection / regression).
  • license_allowlist: optional list of accepted licenses (strings), otherwise not restricted.
  • include_unlabeled: include datasets without labels (default true).
  • prefer_labeled: hint for downstream selection; does not filter by itself.
  • min_valid_image_n_per_dataset: minimum number of valid images per dataset.
  • anatomy_whitelist: restrict by organ keywords (substring match).
  • release_date_min: minimum release year (YYYY or "YYYY-MM-DD").
  • selection.enable: enable Phase 3 constraints. Use min_datasets_per_modality, min_orgs_per_modality; storage_budget_gb is optional (not enforced).
Phase 1&2 keys: dimension, modalities, task_types, license_allowlist, include_unlabeled, prefer_labeled, min_valid_image_n_per_dataset, anatomy_whitelist, release_date_min, allow_3d_as_2d_sources
Phase 3&4 keys: selection.enable, selection.min_datasets_per_modality, selection.min_orgs_per_modality, selection.storage_budget_gb
